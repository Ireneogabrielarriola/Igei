R.A. 9344 - Juvenile Delinquency

R.A. 9344, also known as the "Juvenile Justice and Welfare Act of 2006", is a law in the Philippines that aims to promote and protect the rights of children in conflict with the law.

Key Provisions of R.A. 9344

1. Diversion Program: The law emphasizes the use of diversion programs to address the needs of children in conflict with the law, focusing on their rehabilitation and reintegration into society.

2. Minimum Age of Criminal Responsibility: R.A. 9344 sets the minimum age of criminal responsibility at 15 years old. Children below this age are deemed exempt from criminal liability and are instead subjected to intervention programs.

3. Restorative Justice: The law promotes the use of restorative justice principles, which aim to repair the harm caused by the offense and facilitate the healing and reintegration of the child into the community.

4. Rights of Children: R.A. 9344 recognizes and upholds the rights of children in conflict with the law, including their right to due process, privacy, education, health, and protection from abuse and exploitation.
